What Is Juneteenth

A Day of
Liberation
& Heritage

Juneteenth commemorates June 19, 1865 — the day enslaved people in Texas learned from Union troops that the Civil War had ended and they were free, two and a half years after the Emancipation Proclamation.

Black people in Texas were already resisting slavery from within but were unaware the war had ended. Celebrations abroad use the day to recognize liberation, and to celebrate the culture, achievements, and interconnectedness of the African diaspora.

As the population of Black Americans grows in Barcelona, celebrating Juneteenth provides a new opportunity for the community to ground itself, increase visibility, and become better connected within Barcelona's African diaspora and the city as a whole.

The Place to Be

Why Barcelona?

Barcelona is often celebrated for modernist architecture and Mediterranean beauty. But it was also shaped by deep, complex ties to the African diaspora that stretch far beyond its borders. As a major Mediterranean port, Barcelona has long been a crossroads of people, ideas, and power.

From the medieval period through the rise of the Spanish empire, the city was entangled in transatlantic trade networks that included the trafficking and enslavement of Africans, and the wealth extracted from Black labor in the Caribbean and the Americas. These connections helped finance Barcelona's growth, even as Black lives and contributions were erased from its story.

The Juneteenth Barcelona committee invites you to see Barcelona differently — not just as a European capital, but as a city meaningfully connected to and influenced by the African diaspora. We recognize Barcelona as an important, functional node in Black history.

"By walking these streets with intention, we honor stories that have been cast aside and the people who were forgotten. We uplift the ideals of liberation and pay respect to those who fought — and continue to fight — for freedom."
— Juneteenth Barcelona Committee

Who We Are

The Juneteenth
Committee

Juneteenth Barcelona has an organizing committee of a dozen volunteers dedicated to bringing this celebration to life each year. We are Black Americans, members of the African diaspora, and allies living in Barcelona — united by a shared purpose.

  • 01Connect and inspire Barcelona's African diaspora, creating meaningful space for encounter and belonging.
  • 02Raise our visibility in the city — honoring Black history and culture, and the stories that have been cast aside.
  • 03Support our common liberation, uplifting the ideals of freedom and recognizing Barcelona as a meaningful node in Black history.
